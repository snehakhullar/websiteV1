User Research


To design an easy-to-use interface, pay attention to what users do, not what they say.

Jakob Nielsen

I start designing a product by getting to know the users and understanding their pain-points. I create user personas, scenarios and journey maps to answer foundational questions such as: what problem will this product solve? who will use the product? and why?

Esri Maps for Office

Time frame:July 2013 - Present

Role:UX Architect, Usability Analyst

Webpage link:Click here

Esri Maps for Office (EM4O) is an enterprise solution and one of Esri’s flagship product for driving sales in non-GIS markets. It allows users to easily map their excel data and use analytics to discover spatial patterns.

Objective

User Identification - EM4O had been in-market for 8 years with no clear identification of user profiles.

What I did

  • Used data from customer feedback calls to create Personas such as Ernie Excel and Portia Portal.
  • Identified a back-story, frustrations, motivations and ideal experience for each Persona. Developed a culture of user empathy, enabling the developers and engineers to have better clarity in designing product features.

Impact

  • Reduced a 6-step user process for getting maps to 1 click
  • Received positive user feedback related to more intuitive and user-friendly features
  • Customer comment: "The Esri Maps for Office update is GREAT! Thank you for the improvements.”
